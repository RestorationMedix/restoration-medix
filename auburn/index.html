Water Damage Restoration · Auburn, WA

Local water damage experts
who know Auburn.

Auburn sits at the confluence of the Green River and the White River, which makes it one of King County's highest flood-risk cities. The Green River Valley — running from Auburn north through Kent and Renton — has a long, documented history of flooding, and homes on the valley floor face real risk every rainy season.

But river flooding is only part of the story. Most of the calls we run in Auburn are burst pipes, water heater failures, supply-line leaks, and sewage backups — the everyday emergencies that can soak a home in minutes. Older homes on West Hill and newer builds in Lakeland Hills both deal with their own version of water trouble, from aging galvanized plumbing to crawl space moisture and sump-pump failures.

What we do, the moment you call

A live local dispatcher answers, and a crew is typically rolling to your Auburn address within 60 minutes — any hour, any day. We extract standing water, set up commercial drying equipment, map moisture inside walls and floors with thermal imaging, and treat for mold before it can take hold. Because water sitting for more than 48 hours almost always leads to mold, speed is everything.

And because we're a full general contractor, we don't hand you off after the water's gone. The same crew that dries you out handles reconstruction, roofing, and remodeling — and we document everything to your insurance carrier's standards so your out-of-pocket stays as low as possible.

Auburn FAQ

Water damage questions,

Does Auburn flood often? Which neighborhoods are at highest risk?
Auburn sits where the Green and White Rivers meet, so the valley floor — including Riverside and areas near Hwy 167 — carries genuine flood risk during heavy rain and snowmelt. Hillside neighborhoods like Lea Hill and West Hill flood less from rivers but deal more with stormwater runoff and crawl space moisture. Wherever you are in Auburn, we respond in under 60 minutes.
How fast can you reach Lea Hill or Lakeland Hills?
We keep crews staged locally, so most Auburn calls — including Lea Hill, Lakeland Hills, and the Plateau — get an on-site response within 60 minutes, 24 hours a day.
Will my homeowner's insurance cover water damage in Auburn?
Most sudden, accidental water damage — burst pipes, appliance failures, storm intrusion — is covered by standard homeowner's policies. Gradual leaks and river flooding may need separate coverage. We document everything to carrier standards and bill your insurance directly, so you typically only pay your deductible.
What should I do in the first 10 minutes after a leak?
Shut off the water at the source or your main valve, switch off electricity to affected areas if it's safe, move what you can off wet floors, and call us at (206) 557-6444. The faster extraction starts, the less structural damage and mold you'll face.
Do you handle mold and reconstruction too, or just water removal?
Both. We're a licensed general contractor, so one Auburn crew handles the full job — water extraction, drying, mold remediation, then reconstruction, roofing, and remodeling to put your property back better than before. No subcontractor runaround.
