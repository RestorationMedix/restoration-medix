Water damage restoration
across Federal Way.

Federal Way is a city of lakes and shoreline — Steel Lake, Mirror Lake, Lake Dolloff, North Lake, plus Puget Sound frontage at Dash Point and Redondo. That water is part of what makes the city beautiful, and also part of what puts homes at risk. Lakefront and waterfront properties face higher moisture loads, seasonal high-water events, and the kind of persistent damp that accelerates mold.

Inland, the everyday emergencies are the usual suspects: burst supply lines, failed water heaters, washing-machine hoses, and roof leaks that follow our long rainy stretches. Hylebos Creek and aging neighborhood drainage can add surface-water trouble during big storms.

Then — because we're a licensed general contractor — the same team handles reconstruction, roofing, and remodeling, all documented and billed direct to your insurance carrier.

Federal Way FAQ

Water damage questions,
answered.

Are lakefront homes in Federal Way at higher risk of water damage?
They can be. Homes around Twin Lakes, Steel Lake, Mirror Lake, and Lake Dolloff deal with higher year-round humidity, seasonal high water, and shoreline drainage — all of which raise the odds of moisture intrusion and mold. We're equipped for lakefront and waterfront properties throughout Federal Way.
Do you serve waterfront homes at Dash Point and Redondo?
Yes. We work on Puget Sound bluff and beachfront homes in Dash Point and Redondo, where wind-driven rain and marine moisture create water intrusion challenges that inland homes don't see.
Does my flood insurance cover a burst pipe?
A burst pipe is sudden, accidental water damage and is typically covered by your standard homeowner's policy — not flood insurance. Flood insurance covers rising surface water from storms or overflow. We help document the loss correctly so the right coverage applies.
How fast can you get to Twin Lakes or Adelaide?
Most Federal Way calls — including Twin Lakes, Adelaide, Camelot, and Steel Lake — get an on-site response within 60 minutes, any hour of the day or night.
Will you handle the repairs after drying, or just the cleanup?
We handle everything. As a general contractor, one Federal Way crew extracts the water, dries the structure, remediates mold, and rebuilds — flooring, drywall, cabinetry, paint, and roofing if needed.
