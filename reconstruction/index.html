Reconstruction FAQ

Common questions,
answered.

Do you handle both the mitigation and the rebuild?
Yes — that's the whole point. As a licensed general contractor, the same crew that extracts water and dries your home also handles the full reconstruction. There's no handoff to a separate contractor, no second round of bids, and no gap where things stall.
Will the reconstruction be covered by my insurance?
In most cases, yes. We build our estimate to match your insurer's scope line-for-line and document the work to carrier standards, then bill directly. You typically only pay your deductible plus any upgrades you choose beyond the covered scope.
How long does reconstruction take?
It depends on the extent of the damage — a single room can take a couple of weeks, while a whole-floor rebuild takes longer. We give you a realistic timeline upfront and keep you updated at every stage.
Can you match my existing finishes?
Wherever possible, yes. We source matching or closely comparable flooring, paint, trim, and fixtures so the repaired areas blend with the rest of your home. Where an exact match is discontinued, we'll walk you through the closest options.
