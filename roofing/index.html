Most interior water damage
starts at the roof.

In the Pacific Northwest, your roof takes a beating — months of rain, wind, moss, and the occasional snow load. When a roof starts to fail, the damage rarely stays on the roof. It shows up as stained ceilings, soaked insulation, ruined drywall, and mold in the attic, often long before you spot the actual leak.

Restoration Medix is one of the few roofers who fix both. If your roof is actively leaking, we get an emergency tarp on fast to stop water from reaching your interior. Then we find the real source, document storm or age-related damage for insurance, and repair or replace the roof to code.

Roofing FAQ

Common questions,
answered.

Can you tarp my roof today?
In most cases, yes. Emergency tarping is one of the fastest ways to prevent thousands of dollars in interior water damage, so we prioritize active leaks. Call us and we'll get a crew out to stop the water as quickly as possible.
How do you decide between a repair and a full replacement?
We inspect the roof and attic and weigh the age of the roof, the extent of the damage, and what your insurance covers. A few damaged shingles or a flashing leak is usually a repair; widespread storm damage or an aging roof at end of life often makes replacement the smarter long-term call.
Will insurance cover my roof damage?
Sudden storm and wind damage is often covered by homeowner's insurance, while wear-and-tear from age usually isn't. We document the damage thoroughly and to carrier standards, which gives your claim the best chance of approval. We'll tell you honestly what we see.
Do you also fix the water damage the leak caused?
Yes — and that's what sets us apart. As a restoration general contractor, the same company that repairs your roof also restores the ceilings, insulation, drywall, and any mold the leak caused. No coordinating between a roofer and a separate restoration company.
