Water Damage Restoration · South King County & Pierce County, WA

Every hour of standing water
makes the damage worse.

Water damage is the one emergency where the clock is the enemy. Within minutes, water wicks into drywall, subfloor, and framing. Within 24 to 48 hours, mold begins to grow. The difference between a quick dry-out and a gut-and-rebuild often comes down to how fast a crew arrives.

That's why a live local dispatcher answers your call any hour of the day or night, and a crew is typically rolling to your address within 60 minutes. We handle every cause of water loss across the region — burst and frozen pipes, water heater and appliance failures, supply-line leaks, sewage backups, roof leaks, and storm and river flooding.

Water Damage Restoration FAQ

Common questions,
answered.

How fast can you respond to a water emergency?
We keep crews staged across South King County and Pierce County, so most calls get an on-site response within about 60 minutes — 24 hours a day, including weekends and holidays. The faster we start extraction, the less structural damage and mold you'll face.
Will my insurance cover the water damage?
Most sudden, accidental water damage — burst pipes, appliance failures, storm intrusion — is covered by standard homeowner's policies. Gradual leaks and flooding may need separate coverage. We document everything to carrier standards and bill your insurance directly, so you typically only pay your deductible.
How long does drying take?
Most homes dry in 3 to 5 days, though it depends on how much water there was, what materials got wet, and how long it sat. We take daily moisture readings and only pull the equipment once the structure meets dry standards — not before.
Do you handle mold and the rebuild too, or just water removal?
All of it. As a licensed general contractor, one crew handles water extraction, drying, mold remediation, and full reconstruction — drywall, flooring, paint, and more. No subcontractor runaround between the cleanup and the repair.
